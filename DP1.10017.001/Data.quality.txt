Assessing the quality of digital hemispherical photos (DHPs) involves inspecting images for sharp focus, masking out large tree boles close to the camera lens that may inflate estimates of Plant Area Index, and for downward-facing images, masking out the photographer's legs and feet. Image focus may be assessed manually or programmatically (e.g., using Python OpenCV or equivalent).
